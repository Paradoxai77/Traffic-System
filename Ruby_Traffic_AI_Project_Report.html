01 · OVERVIEW

What is Ruby Traffic AI?

Ruby Traffic AI is a full-stack smart city traffic management platform built for real-world hackathon demonstration. It combines a cyberpunk-themed React dashboard with a Python Flask REST API and an independent Edge AI simulation engine to deliver live, actionable traffic intelligence — all from a single browser window.

Core Idea: Instead of passive CCTV monitoring, Ruby Traffic AI actively analyses traffic density, detects violations, predicts congestion, and triggers emergency overrides — all autonomously and in real time.

06 · API REFERENCE

REST Endpoints

The Flask backend runs on http://localhost:5000 and exposes two endpoints:

GET
/api/dashboard_data
Returns the full traffic state as JSON — intersections, violations, alerts, historical density, system status, environment stats, and total violations count. Polled by the React frontend every 2 seconds.
POST
/api/traffic_update
Accepts a JSON payload from the Edge AI simulation engine. Supports partial updates — can include any combination of: intersections, violation, alert, historical_density, environment. Violations are capped at 10 entries; alerts at 5.
Sample violation payload fields: id, type (Red Light Jump / Over-speeding / Wrong Way / Jaywalking), plate, location, time, confidence (%), speed (km/h), vehicle class, fine ($)
Sample alert payload fields: id, severity (critical / high / medium), message, location, time, action_taken, model_conf (%)

07 · EDGE AI ENGINE

Edge AI Simulation

The Edge AI Engine (edge/sim.py) is a standalone Python script that mimics what a real edge compute node would do — analysing camera feeds and sensor data locally before sending aggregated results to the central platform.

🔄 5-Second Cycle
Every 5 seconds, all 4 intersection densities are updated via a random walk (±8%). Weather events increase congestion by +2 to +5.
🌦 Weather Engine
5% per-cycle chance of weather change (CLEAR / RAIN / FOG). RAIN & FOG reduce base speed from 45 to 25 km/h and increase density.
🚨 EVP Logic
3% per-cycle chance of emergency vehicle override. Signal instantly goes green, wait time resets. 30% chance to clear each subsequent cycle.
📸 ANPR Engine
30% per-cycle chance of violation event. Randomly selects type, vehicle class, speed, plate. Calculates fine: Red Light $250, Wrong Way $500, Over-speeding $150+, Jaywalking $50.
🔔 Alert Engine
15% per-cycle chance of hazard alert. Four hazard types: Heavy Collision, Stranded Vehicle, Road Debris, Wrong Way Driver. Each has a predefined automated response action.
🌿 CO₂ Tracking
Each successful smart signal switch from red → green (density relief or wait > 60s) saves 1–4 kg of CO₂ and adds to the cumulative counter.

Artificial Intelligence offers vast potential in revolutionising traffic management systems. The use of AI is based on real-time data collection and analysis — using machine learning, computer vision, and data analytics to give traffic planners actionable insights to analyse, detect, and predict traffic patterns.

Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS): AI forms the backbone of modern ITS, where vehicles, infrastructure, and control centres share information via Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) communication — V2V, V2I, and V2P — improving awareness, optimising signal timing, and reducing congestion.

13 · USE CASES

8 Proven AI Traffic Use Cases

USE CASE 01
🔮 Traffic Flow Prediction
By analysing real-time and historical data, AI predicts where congestion will form and helps cities act before delays build up. ML algorithms learn and identify patterns between variables that impact traffic flow.
City adjusts traffic lights in advance before a stadium event to prevent bottlenecks forming.
USE CASE 02
⚠️ Incident Detection & Management
AI spots unusual road events within seconds, allowing traffic operators to respond immediately. Rapid detection prevents long delays and reduces secondary accidents caused by stationary vehicles.
Cameras identify a wrong-way driver on a highway and alert authorities instantly. Stopped truck detected → drivers rerouted in real-time.
USE CASE 03
🚦 Adaptive Signal Control
AI-powered signals use Coordinated Progressive Signaling (constant flow at busy intersections) and Coordinated Adaptive Signals (real-time demand alignment) — replacing static signal timing.
Spike in vehicles → longer green phases. Late at night → shorter red phases to avoid unnecessary waiting.
USE CASE 04
🚌 Public Transport Optimization
AI analyses how people move — identifying busiest bus stops, demand spikes, and typical delays. The system continuously adjusts timetables and redesigns routes when needed instead of relying on static schedules.
Consistently overcrowded 8AM bus line gets an extra vehicle added. Under-used routes auto-shortened.
USE CASE 05
🚨 Emergency Vehicle Preemption
AI creates a "green wave" by adjusting nearby traffic lights to give emergency vehicles a clear route. Cameras and sensors detect the approaching vehicle, cascading green signals along its path — every second matters.
✅ Implemented in Ruby Traffic AI — 3% per-cycle EVP trigger with 30% auto-clear logic per cycle.
USE CASE 06
🌿 Environmental Monitoring
AI combines data from cameras, pollution sensors, and vehicle classifications (cars, trucks, electric, diesel) to understand how traffic impacts emissions and redirect flows away from sensitive zones like schools or hospitals.
✅ Ruby Traffic AI tracks CO₂ saved in real time as signal optimisations reduce idle engine time.
USE CASE 07
🅿️ Smart Parking Management
AI monitors parking lots and street parking via camera analytics in real time — sending availability data to navigation apps, road signs, or in-car systems to eliminate search traffic circling.
Jackson Hole Airport: video analytics monitored occupancy, reducing wait times and guiding drivers to spots instantly.
USE CASE 08
🗺️ AI Navigation & Route Optimization
AI-powered navigation apps leverage real-time traffic data from GPS signals, sensors, and user-generated data for dynamic rerouting. Variable Speed Limits (VSL) can be congestion-responsive or weather-responsive, connected with ride-sharing, parking, and EV charging ecosystems.
Dynamic rerouting of 10,000 drivers around an accident: avg delay reduced from 25 min to under 3 min.

14 · AI TECHNOLOGY

The Technology Behind Traffic AI

🤖 Machine Learning Models
Trained on historical databases to learn and identify patterns between variables impacting traffic flow. Primary advantage: can analyse increased complexity and non-linear relationships to generate predictions of enhanced accuracy. Algorithms automatically detect and adapt to real-time patterns.
📡 Multi-Source Data Fusion
Predictions use several data sources simultaneously: historical records, weather data, GPS traces, existing road network information, real-time camera-based video analytics, and IoT sensors — combined for maximum reliability.
👁️ Computer Vision & Video Analytics
Transforms ordinary camera streams into actionable traffic insights: detecting vehicle movements, counting flows, identifying congestion, and recognising abnormal situations. Works completely privacy-compliant — objects and humans are always anonymised and pixelated. No new hardware required.
🪞 Digital Twins
Virtual replicas of physical traffic assets calibrated to different KPIs. Allow simulation of interventions — rerouting, adjusting lights, deploying warnings — before gridlock forms. Aachen–Düsseldorf: 4.3 km, 100+ lidars, 200 cameras, live digital twin. Fully GDPR-compliant.
🌐 V2X Communication
Vehicle-to-Everything (V2V, V2I, V2P) enables vehicles, infrastructure, and control centres to share safety-critical data in real time. Connected vehicles transmit speed, position, and braking data to predict potential conflicts and support smoother, safer traffic flow.
⚡ Variable Speed Limits (VSL)
Dynamic speed limits aligned to congestion or weather conditions. When congestion builds, VSL slows upstream traffic to prevent shockwaves. Hard Shoulder Running (HSR) allows temporary use of hard shoulders as extra driving lanes during peak demand — AI predicts demand surges reliably.

16 · CHALLENGES

Key Challenges to Address

AI offers powerful opportunities but its use brings important challenges that cities must address proactively:

01
🔒 Data Privacy
Data privacy standards must be stringently maintained. Anonymisation of data streams protects identity and financial information. Privacy masks are applicable to any detected object automatically. All AI-powered applications must comply with GDPR (EU) and CCPA (US). Ruby Traffic AI's ANPR data is a prime area requiring privacy-first design in production deployments.
02
🛡 Cybersecurity
Data security safeguards digital data against cyber attacks or data breaches. Key measures include multi-factor authentication, end-to-end encryption, and firewall protection. When combined, they significantly enhance how secure the traffic management data pipeline is against exposure, deletion, or corruption.
03
⚖️ Ethical Decision-Making
The human component is crucial for ensuring ethical principles are upheld when making important decisions. True success with AI requires a judicious mixture of human judgment and machine intelligence — especially critical when AI decisions affect emergency response prioritisation and traffic violation enforcement.
04
🤝 Public Acceptance & Trust
AI in traffic can be fully implemented only when accepted by all stakeholders — personnel, planners, drivers, commuters, law enforcement, and the general public. Awareness is imperative, as is positive reinforcement through consequence-based decisions. Transparency in how AI makes decisions is critical for long-term adoption and public confidence.
